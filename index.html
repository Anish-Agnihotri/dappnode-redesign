What's all this about?

What happens when you want to use a decentralized P2P network, but securing your own node takes a lot of time and effort? You have a decentralization bottleneck at the entry point, which is where we are now in the blockchain space.


Problem

  1. At no point in the last two years has there been more than 13,000 Bitcoin nodes online, and that number got as low as 5,500.
  2. MetaMask, MyEtherWallet, and MyCrypto are the primary wallets used on tEthereum, and most users are using these wallets' default nodes. This is a huge centralizing force in Ethereum.
  3. The people who do run their own node end up having to spend a lot of time troubleshooting, keeping it updated, and synced without the ability to easily share their node (and efforts) with family and friends.

Solution

  1. DAppNode will address these issues by creating a simple, easy, self-empowering system made specifically for hosting P2P clients for DApps, Cryptocurrencies, VPNs, IPFS, and more.
  2. Members of the WHG have spent countless hours developing DAppNode because they believe it to be the most important and pertinent censorship resistant tool, to help bring the decentralized world everyone wants for themselves, their family, and their friends.
